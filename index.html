All About Evan Quist!

On Campus

Evan Quist is a Computer Science and Finance double major at Illinois Wesleyan University! Currently Evan is in his Sophomore year, and works as an RA on Campus in addition to his job as a 'Titan Ambassador' at the admissions office, where he helps recruit Computer Science and Finance students to the university. He also serves as the Association for Computing Machinery (ACM) chapter Vice President.

Off Campus

When he is not at school working on homework and studying for his exams Evan can be found walking the halls of State Farm Insurance, where he works as a software development intern. He began his career there in 2017 as a High School Intern, and has continued there since. He has been fortunate enough to work in several departments, accruing an immense amount of work experience in varying fields.

  1. Information Security June 2017 - December 2017

    His first assignment was to work as a Information Security focused software developer, during which time he created apps that automated security access provisioning and allowed for quicker auditing.

  2. Solutions Engineering & Testing June 2018 - August 2018

    Learning from his experiences, Evan was assigned to assist with an experimental project that combined six interns into a single team to create production applications. The team was one of the first of its kind at State Farm and serves as a model for software development teams in the internship program today. During his time with Team WinTerns (as they named themselves) Evan was given the chance to learn React.JS and develop a web application that could dynamically fetch information about different resources provisioned to test areas throughout State Farm. It was during this time first began to work with Unit and Integration tests, and served as a Scrum Master in addition to his work as a developer.

  3. Search Engine Optimization & Search Engine Marketing June 2019 - Present

    At the begining of the 2019 internship program Evan was approached by his manager, the person responsible for overseeing the Enterprise technology intern program in Bloomington, to be placed in an area that usually did not request interns. Evan, very interested in learning something new, accepted the offer and joined the Search Team at the begining of June 2019. During this time, Evan lead an Enterprise Wide project known as Product Local Landing Pages, in which State Farm generated unique localized content for SEO on over 6,000 pages located in cities across the United States. The pages drove thousands of quotes to State Farm, and Evan was given an award by the manager of the Search Team for his role in leading the effort. Evan is currently continuing his work with the team by leading a Page Speed Improvement effort across the entire StateFarm.com website.
